## Full 45-minute outline

| Time | Act | Beat | Duration | What happens |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 0:00 | 1 | Hook | 2m | Question the audience has lived. No slides. No product. "Who's been paged between 2 and 5am in the last month?" |
| 0:02 | 1 | Gap | 3m | What today's tools cover vs what they do not. Name real tables / tools / teams. Two or three gaps max. |
| 0:05 | 1 | Cost | 3m | Quantify impact per persona. Use real incident references: "INC-4471 took 6h to diagnose because…". |
| 0:08 | 2 | Product | 10m | Show output first, machinery later. Each capability callback: "Remember the gap where X? Here's what it looks like now." |
| 0:18 | 2 | Comparison | 5m | Address "why not X?" head-on. Acknowledge X's strengths. Show what X cannot do. "Complementary, not replacement." |
| 0:23 | 3 | Live demo | 8m | One happy path. Narrate the WHY, not the WHAT. Time-box strictly. Have a fallback screenshot sequence. |
| 0:31 | 3 | One more thing | 2m | Change or add something mid-demo to show flexibility. Keeps the "it's just a canned demo" objection away. |
| 0:33 | 3 | Close the loop | 2m | Path from demo to production in 3–4 concrete steps. Not "contact us". |
| 0:35 | 4 | Use cases | 3m | 2–3 persona-specific before/after scenarios. One sentence each. |
| 0:38 | 4 | Getting started | 2m | Step table: What / Who. Show current adoption numbers if non-zero. |
| 0:40 | 5 | Q&A | 5m | Open floor. Short + Detailed answers prepped. "Yes, and…" not "but…". |

## Variant A: Greenfield

**Use when**: demoing a capability no team owns today.

- **Act 1 pivot**: the pain is ambient and under-attributed ("we all just accept this"). Spend extra time on Cost — greenfield audiences have not priced the pain.
- **Act 2 pivot**: heavier Product (12m), lighter Comparison (3m) — there is no direct competitor, so compare to the manual workflow.
- **Act 3 pivot**: the "one more thing" beat matters more here. Greenfield audiences need to see flexibility or they assume it is a one-trick demo.
- **Act 4 pivot**: Use Cases beat leans on multiple personas to show breadth.

Common failure: burying the pain because it is ambient. Fix: open with one specific person's story, not an aggregate.

## Variant B: Retrofit

**Use when**: extending or replacing an internal tool teams already use.

- **Act 1 pivot**: lead with "here's what $tool does well" before the gap. Skip this and the audience defends their tool before you finish Act 1.
- **Act 2 pivot**: Comparison beat expands to 7m, Product shrinks to 8m. The meat of the sell is "here is what your current tool cannot do."
- **Act 3 pivot**: live demo must include data or a workflow from the existing tool. "This is the same table you're looking at in $tool today, with what we add on top."
- **Act 4 pivot**: Getting Started must include a migration path. If it is "rebuild from scratch", retrofit is the wrong frame — pitch it as greenfield instead.

Common failure: dismissing the incumbent. Fix: name what the incumbent genuinely does better and say you are not replacing that part.

## Variant C: Migration

**Use when**: asking teams to move off a legacy system on a deadline.

- **Act 1 pivot**: add a "why now" beat — compliance date, EOL, cost cliff. Without it, the audience asks "why this quarter?"
- **Act 2 pivot**: Product beat shows **feature parity first** (not new features). Migrations fail when the team suspects regressions.
- **Act 3 pivot**: live demo runs the audience's own workload, not a toy example. Pre-load their data.
- **Act 4 pivot**: Getting Started is a migration checklist with owner, date, and rollback plan per step.

Common failure: leading with new features. Fix: parity first, delta second. Earn trust that nothing regresses before selling what's new.

## Transition phrases that work

| From | To | Phrase |
|---|---|---|
| Act 1 Hook | Act 1 Gap | "That's the question. Here's what's actually covered today — and what isn't." |
| Act 1 Cost | Act 2 Product | "So that's the world we live in. Here's the one we could live in instead." |
| Act 2 Product | Act 2 Comparison | "Before I go further — I know someone's thinking 'why not just use X?'" |
| Act 2 Comparison | Act 3 Live demo | "Enough slides. Let me show you." |
| Act 3 Demo | Act 3 One more thing | "One more thing — watch what happens when I change this." |
| Act 3 | Act 4 | "So that's the product. Here's what it changes for three specific teams." |
| Act 4 | Act 5 | "That's the pitch. What are you thinking?" |

## Timing discipline

- Rehearse with a timer. A 45-minute demo rehearsed once runs 55 minutes live.
- If Act 3 slips past its 12 minutes, cut from Act 2, never from Act 5. Q&A is where the audience decides.
- If Act 1 slips past 8 minutes, the hook or gap is muddled — rewrite, do not truncate.
- Hard rule: if the cheat sheet does not fit on one page, the demo is too long.
